What is the buy on the dip investment strategy? "Buy the dips" refers to buying an asset after its value has declined.The belief is that the new lower price constitutes a bargain because the "dip" is simply a temporary (This a BIG TRAP at times) dip and the asset will rebound and improve in value over time. Buying the dips refers to consistently buying an asset or security after it has seen a short-term fall. In long-term uptrends, buying the dips can be beneficial, but in secular downtrends, it is unprofitable or more difficult.Dip purchasing can reduce one's average cost of ownership of a stock, but the risk and benefit of dip buying should be assessed on a regular basis. Buying the dips, like any other trading strategy, does not guarantee gains. A reduction in the value of an asset can occur for a variety of reasons, including changes in its underlying worth.Just because something is less expensive than it was before doesn't mean it's a good deal.The issue is that the average investor has limited capacity to tell the difference between a momentary price decrease and a warning indication that prices are about to fall dramatically.
